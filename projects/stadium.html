Stadium Design & Scale Model

A conceptual stadium project exploring structural efficiency, architectural form, and spectator experience through digital modeling and physical fabrication.

Project Overview

This project involved designing a conceptual stadium and transforming the design into a physical scale model. The goal was to balance structural efficiency, visual appearance, and the overall spectator experience.

Design Objectives

The project focused on understanding the challenges associated with large-span structures, seating arrangements, circulation, and overall stadium layout. Special attention was given to creating a design that was both functional and visually appealing.

Model Development

The stadium was designed in SolidWorks as a complete digital model before fabrication began. The design process included developing the overall geometry, seating configuration, structural framing concepts, and architectural features.


After the digital model was completed, components were manufactured using 3D printing and laser cutting techniques before being assembled by hand into the final scale model.

Fabrication Process

The project required coordination between digital design and physical construction. Individual components were prepared for fabrication, manufactured using multiple methods, and carefully assembled to create a detailed representation of the final stadium concept.
