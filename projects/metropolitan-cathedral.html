Metropolitan Cathedral Structural Model

A detailed scale model studying architectural form, foundation behavior, and the structural challenges of building on unstable soil.

Project Overview

This project focused on creating a detailed scale model of the Metropolitan Cathedral while studying the structural challenges related to unstable and sinking soil conditions.


The project combined architectural modeling, fabrication planning, and structural thinking to better understand how large historic structures respond to difficult foundation conditions.

Design Objective

The objective was to recreate the cathedral in a physical model while also highlighting the engineering issues connected to foundation settlement, soil instability, and long-term structural behavior.

Modeling & Fabrication Process

The model was first developed in 3D using SolidWorks. Additional parts were prepared using Onshape and Adobe Illustrator to create pieces suitable for laser cutting and 3D printing.


After fabrication, the model was assembled by hand. This process required planning each component, checking fit between pieces, and balancing visual detail with constructability.

Engineering Focus

A major focus of the project was understanding how unstable soil can affect large structures. The project explored foundation behavior, architectural resilience, and possible reinforcement strategies that could help address movement or settlement over time.
