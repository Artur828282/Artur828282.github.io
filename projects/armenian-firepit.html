Armenian Firepit

A commemorative fabrication project inspired by Tsitsernakabert, the Armenian Genocide Memorial, designed to honor resilience, remembrance, and cultural heritage.

Project Overview

This project involved the design and fabrication of a commemorative firepit inspired by Tsitsernakabert, the Armenian Genocide Memorial located in Yerevan, Armenia.


The goal was to create a smaller-scale interpretation of the monument while preserving its symbolic meaning. The finished piece serves as a tribute to remembrance, resilience, and Armenian cultural identity.

Design Process

The project began with researching the original monument and identifying key visual elements that could be adapted into a fabricated steel design.


CAD drawings were created to develop the geometry, dimensions, and fabrication plan before manufacturing began.

Fabrication Process

Steel components were cut using a shear machine and prepared for assembly. The individual pieces were then TIG welded together to create the final structure.


Care was taken to maintain dimensional accuracy and achieve clean welds while preserving the intended appearance of the design.
