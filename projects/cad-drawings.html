Manufacturing Drawings Archive

A collection of manufacturing, fabrication, reverse engineering, and assembly drawings developed to support production, accuracy, and engineering communication.

Project Overview

This archive contains manufacturing and fabrication drawings developed to support production, assembly, reverse engineering, and quality control activities. The collection demonstrates technical drafting, CAD modeling, manufacturing communication, and the ability to transform engineering concepts into production-ready documentation.


The drawings shown here were created for real-world manufacturing and fabrication processes, ranging from custom fixture design to reverse engineered components and assembly documentation.

Featured Drawings

Welding Fixture Design

Engineered and designed a custom welding fixture used in the shop to improve part accuracy, consistency, and repeatability. The fixture was developed to help prevent welding defects by holding components in the correct position during fabrication.

Spray Nozzle Reverse Engineering

Reverse engineered an existing spray nozzle component by measuring the original part, recreating the geometry in CAD, and producing a technical drawing for documentation and manufacturing reference.

Wire Harness Assembly

Created assembly documentation for a wire harness system, showing component organization, assembly relationships, and the information needed to support production and proper installation.

Valve Assembly

Developed assembly documentation for a valve system, identifying component relationships, assembly requirements, and manufacturing details needed for production and maintenance.

Engineering Importance

Engineering drawings are the bridge between design and manufacturing. A successful drawing must communicate dimensions, tolerances, materials, assembly requirements, and fabrication details clearly enough for accurate production.


This project demonstrates the ability to create documentation that supports shop work, quality control, and clear communication between engineering and fabrication teams.
